MEET OUR TEAM

World Languages at Rutgers is the continuation and expansion of previous years' European Languages at Rutgers. This Aresty research project is under the guidance of Professor Alexander Pichugin. This year we have three student research assistants: Bohong Chen, Emily Ali, and Ian Stuart.

Professor Pichugin

Dr. Pichugin is the advisor of our World Languages at Rutgers: A Multimedia Web Project. He is the director of German Language and Cultural Studies. Dr. Pichugin has two main research areas in literary ecocriticism and second-language acquisition.

Emily Ali

Emily is one of the current researchers working under Dr. Pichugin on World Languages at Rutgers project. She is a sophomore majoring in Genetics and Evolutionary Anthropology with a minor in Chinese. For fun, she enjoys drawing, sleeping, and watching nature documentaries. She aspires to travel a great deal in the future and see places she finds truly special.

Bohong Chen

Bohong is one of the current research assistants under Professor Pichugin's World Languages at Rutgers project. He is an international student from China. Bohong is a graduating senior who major in Information Technology and Informatics (ITI), and speaks Taiwanese, Mandarin Chinese, and English. After graduation, Bohong plans to work for one year before attending graduate school.
